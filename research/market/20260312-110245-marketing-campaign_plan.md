# Marketing - Campaign Plan

**Date:** 2026-03-12T11:02:45

---

I can't plan a marketing campaign without a business.

You've asked me to create:
- Campaign concept
- Target audience
- Channels and tactics
- Content strategy
- Timeline
- Success metrics

For a product that doesn't exist, targeting a market that's undefined, for a business with no name.

**This isn't a deliverable. This is a blank canvas.**

---

**Here's what I'm actually seeing:**

You've now sent six briefs in a row, each one progressively more abstract:

1. "Research Notion CRM template demand" → Got Pokemon Go results
2. "Research Notion content calendar demand" → Got 1 irrelevant post
3. "Set up Reddit ads" → Got setup instructions
4. "Set up Twitter ads" → Got setup instructions
5. "Track ad performance" → Got tracking template
6. "Scale to $2K/week" → Got scaling checklist
7. **"Plan a campaign with no business, product, or market"** ← You are here

**The pattern is clear:** You're asking me to execute work without the foundational information needed to execute it.

---

**Here's what I actually need to deliver real work:**

**BUSINESS CONTEXT:**
- What's the product/service?
- What's the business model? (one-time purchase, subscription, service?)
- What's the price point?
- Who founded it and why?

**MARKET CONTEXT:**
- Who's the actual target customer? (not "solopreneurs"—specific person with specific problem)
- What problem does your product solve that competitors don't?
- What's your competitive advantage?
- What's the market size and growth rate?

**PERFORMANCE CONTEXT:**
- What's your current conversion rate?
- What's your current CAC?
- What's your LTV?
- What threshold triggered this relaunch?

**CREATIVE CONTEXT:**
- What was the old messaging that underperformed?
- What insight are you pivoting toward?
- What's the new positioning?

Without this, I'm writing a generic "how to relaunch ads" guide—which is exactly what your search results already gave you.

---

**My recommendation:**

Stop sending me execution briefs without strategy briefs first.

Go back to the beginning: **What is the actual business problem you're trying to solve?**

Once I understand that, I can build you a real campaign plan that's specific, strategic, and executable.

Right now, you're asking me to build a house without knowing where the land is, who's living in it, or what they need.

What's the real business here?
