- 60% to performance marketing (paid ads for quick validations, conversions, measurable ROI)

- Paid strengths: Fast results, conversions, retention

**Industry Context** [2]:

- Avoid if funnel requires immediate conversions over trust-building

## My Actual Recommendation

**This is a solid budget allocation strategy for the right scenario. But it's not universal.**

### When 60/40 Works

**You should shift to 60% paid / 40% organic if:**

1. **You're a growing brand with strong organic potential** [1][2]
   - Your content is generating 3x more leads at 62% lower cost
   - You have SEO ranking potential
   - You can produce content at scale

2. **Your organic is currently underfunded** [1]
   - You're currently at 40% paid / 10% organic
   - Organic is outperforming paid
   - You have untapped organic channels

3. **Your funnel rewards trust-building** [2]
   - B2B SaaS (not D2C ecommerce)
   - Sales cycle >30 days
   - Requires education and trust
   - High customer lifetime value

4. **You can tolerate a 1-3 year ramp** [3]
   - You have runway
   - You're not chasing immediate revenue
   - You can sustain paid while organic compounds

### When 60/40 Doesn't Work

**You should NOT shift to 60% paid / 40% organic if:**

1. **Your industry skews heavily paid** [2]
   - D2C ecommerce
   - High-velocity, low-consideration purchases
   - Seasonal or time-sensitive products

2. **Your funnel requires immediate conversions** [2]
   - You need revenue now
   - Sales cycle is <7 days
   - Low customer lifetime value

3. **Your organic is underperforming** [1]
   - Your content isn't generating leads
   - You have no SEO ranking potential
   - You can't produce content at scale

4. **You don't have runway** [3]
   - You need profitability in 90 days
   - You can't sustain paid while organic ramps
   - You're cash-constrained
