### PHASE 1: AUDIT & PREPARE (Days 1-3)

**Step 1: Analyze Current KPIs**[1][2][4]

Pull data on all active campaigns:
- CTR (click-through rate) - target: >1%[1]
- CPC (cost per click) - lower is better[1]
- CPA (cost per acquisition) - target: profitable[1][2][4][5]
- ROAS (return on ad spend) - target: >3:1[1][2]
- Conversion rate - target: >2%[1]

**Step 2: Identify Winners vs. Underperformers**[1][2]

**Winners:** High CTR + Low CPA + Strong ROAS
- Keep these. You'll scale these.

**Underperformers:** Low CTR + High CPA + Weak ROAS
- Pause these. Don't scale losers.[1][2]

**Step 3: Consolidate Winners via CBO**[1][2][4]

Use Campaign Budget Optimization (CBO) to:
- Combine top-performing ad sets under one campaign
- Let Meta/Facebook algorithm allocate budget to best performers
- Simplifies scaling (one budget to increase, not multiple)[1][2][4]

**Step 4: Verify Landing Page Performance**[1][2][4]

Check conversion rates on landing pages:
- Target: >3% conversion rate[1][2][4]
- If lower, optimize before scaling (scaling bad landing pages wastes budget)[1][2][4]

**Key rules for vertical scaling:**[1][4][5][6]

### PHASE 3: HORIZONTAL SCALING (Week 3+)

**Strategy:** Expand reach by testing new audiences + fresh creatives[1][2][3][4]

**Tactic 1: Duplicate Top Ad Sets for New Audiences**[1][2][3][4]

Take your best-performing ad set and duplicate it targeting:
- Lookalike audiences (similar to best customers)[1][2][3][4]
- New interest-based audiences[1][2][3][4]
- New geographic regions[1][2][3][4]
- New device types (mobile vs. desktop)[1][2][3][4]

**Expected result:** Reach new customers without saturating existing audience[1][2][3][4]

**Tactic 2: Test Fresh Creatives**[1][2][3][4]

Refresh ads weekly to prevent fatigue:[1][2][3][4]
- New video creative
- New copy variations
- New images
- New hooks/angles

**Why:** Audiences get tired of seeing the same ad. Fresh creative = lower frequency needed, lower CPA[1][2][3][4]

**Tactic 3: Add Retargeting**[1][2][3][4]

Target warm audiences (people who've already engaged):
- Website visitors who didn't convert
- People who watched your video
- People who engaged with your posts
- Past customers

**Expected result:** Higher conversion rates, lower CPA on warm audiences[1][2][3][4]
