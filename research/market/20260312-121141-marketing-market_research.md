## CUSTOMER INTERVIEW PLAYBOOK: TOP 15 PAYING CUSTOMERS

### THE CORE FRAMEWORK

**Goal:** Understand what your best customers actually value, what problems they face, and what they'd pay for next.

**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks[1][6]

**Format:** 30-60 minute Zoom calls[1][3]

---

### STEP 1: IDENTIFY YOUR TOP 15

**Source:** Your billing or CRM data[1]

**Priority order:**
1. Highest lifetime value customers[1]
2. Recent high spenders[1]
3. Long-term retention (been with you 6+ months)[1]

**Output:** List of 15 names + email addresses + purchase history

---

### STEP 2: RECRUIT THEM

**Email template:**

Subject: "Quick conversation about your experience with [product]"

Body:
"Hi [Name],

You're one of our most valued customers, and I'd love to spend 30-45 minutes learning about your experience with [product].

This isn't a sales call—I want to understand what's working, what's not, and what would help you succeed even more.

Would you be open to a quick Zoom call next week? I'm flexible on timing.

Thanks,
[Your name]"

**Add:** NDA offer if discussing sensitive topics[3]

**Expected response rate:** 60-70% of top customers will say yes[1]

---

### STEP 3: PREPARE YOUR INTERVIEW GUIDE

**Use this 5-question script (proven framework):**[5]

1. **"What's the hardest part about [their main task / your product category]?"**
   - Listen for pain points, frustrations, workarounds

2. **"Can you tell me about the last time that happened?"**
   - Get specific example, not abstract complaint

3. **"Why was that hard?"**
   - Dig into root cause

4. **"What, if anything, have you done to solve that problem?"**
   - Understand their current solutions (yours + competitors)

5. **"What don't you love about the solutions you've tried (including ours)?"**
   - Get honest feedback on what's missing

**Add rapport-builders at the start:**[3]
- "How long have you been using our product?"
- "How did you first hear about us?"

**Follow-up probes:**[1][2]
- "Can you expand on that?"
- "Why?"
- "Tell me more about that"

---

### STEP 4: CONDUCT THE INTERVIEWS

**Team setup:** 2 people[1][2]
- **Facilitator:** Asks questions, listens, probes
- **Observer:** Takes notes on verbal + non-verbal cues (hesitations, body language, tone shifts)[1][2]

**During the call:**
- Let them talk 80-90% of the time[1][2][4]
- Don't pitch or defend your product[1][2]
- Practice "radical listening"—catch offhand comments[1][2][4]
- Validate: "So it sounds like..." and repeat back[3][6]
- Record with permission (anonymize notes later)[1][6]

**Debrief immediately after each call:**[1][6]
- What surprised you?
- What pain point came up?
- What opportunity did you hear?
- What should we follow up on?

---

### STEP 5: ANALYZE PATTERNS

**After all 15 interviews, debrief as a team:**[1]

**Categorize by:**
- Pain points (what problems keep coming up?)[1]
- Behaviors (how do they actually use your product?)[1]
- Opportunities (what would they pay for next?)[1]
- Unmet needs (what are they solving with other tools?)[1]

**Watch for:**
- Patterns across 3+ customers (signal, not noise)[1]
- Offhand comments that reveal real problems[1][2][4]
- Gaps between what they say and what they do[1][2]

**Output:** 1-page summary of top 3-5 insights[1]

---

### STEP 6: BUILD RELATIONSHIPS

**At the end of each call:**[5][6]
- Thank them for their time
- Ask for referrals to other customers
- Ask permission for future contact
- Offer to build an advisory board (quarterly check-ins)[5][6]

## THE QUESTIONS YOU SHOULD ASK

**Beyond the core 5, consider these:**[1][3][4][5]

**About their workflow:**
- "Walk me through how you use [product] on a typical day"
- "What's the first thing you do when you log in?"
- "What features do you use most?"

**About alternatives:**
- "What were you using before [product]?"
- "Why did you switch to us?"
- "What would make you consider switching again?"

**About value:**
- "What's the biggest win you've gotten from [product]?"
- "How has [product] changed your business?"
- "What would happen if you didn't have [product]?"

**About expansion:**
- "What's the next problem you'd like to solve?"
- "What would you pay for [feature X]?"
- "Who else in your organization should be using this?"
