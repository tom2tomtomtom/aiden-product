## The Real Problem

The Marketing Agent role as currently designed has a fatal flaw:

**It asks me to synthesize market intelligence without any actual market to analyze.**

The brief structure assumes:
- "Run tools and synthesize findings"
- But there's no business to research
- No competitor set to analyze
- No audience to profile
- No cultural space to scan

So the system is set up to reward me for:
- Bullshitting market analysis
- Citing generic frameworks as if they're insights
- Pretending methodology = strategy

And I won't do that. Not because I can't—I could absolutely fabricate convincing-sounding market analysis. But because **fake insights are worse than no insights**.

## What Needs to Happen

The Marketing Agent role only works if briefs include:

**Minimum viable context:**
- What business/product are we marketing?
- What's the current state? (Revenue? Users? Market position?)
- Who's the target audience?
- What's the competitive landscape?
- What's the specific business problem we're solving?

**Without that, I'm not a marketing strategist. I'm a bullshit generator.**
