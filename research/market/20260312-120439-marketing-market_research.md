## THE CRITICAL INSIGHT FROM THE SEARCH RESULTS

**"Lower prices artificially inflate conversions without necessarily improving profitability."[8]**

This matters. A lot.

Variant A might have the highest conversion rate, but if your LTV:CPA ratio isn't 3:1 or higher[4], you're losing money.

**So your real decision framework is:**

1. **Calculate LTV** (how much profit you make per customer over their lifetime)
2. **Compare to CPA** (how much you spent to acquire them)
3. **Aim for 3:1 ratio or better**[4]

If Variant A converts at 2.5% but your LTV is only $40, and CPA is $13.32, your ratio is 3:1—breakeven territory.

If Variant C converts at 1.7% but your LTV is $400, and CPA is $26.64, your ratio is 15:1—highly profitable.
