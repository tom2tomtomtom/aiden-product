The Objections That Kill Deals

These aren't price objections. These are trust objections. And you're probably answering them wrong.

"It's Too Expensive"

They're not saying the price is high. They're saying they don't see enough value yet. Your ROI story is weak.

"We Need To Think About It"

Translation: "I'm not convinced this is urgent." You've created interest, not urgency. The window closes while they think.

"Your Competitor Does It Better"

They've done research but haven't understood your differentiation. You're playing their game instead of your game.

"We Don't Have Budget Right Now"

Budget exists for urgent problems. You haven't made this feel urgent enough to reallocate resources.

"This Doesn't Fit Our Workflow"

They're protecting their current system. You haven't shown the pain of staying where they are.

"We're Happy With What We Have"

Contentment is the hardest objection. You need to create productive dissatisfaction, not attack their current choice.

The Objection Framework That Works

Every objection has a pattern. Every pattern has a counter-pattern. We've mapped them all.

Step 1: Acknowledge - Show you heard the real concern, not just the words.

Step 2: Reframe - Shift the conversation from their frame to your frame.

Step 3: Prove - Provide specific evidence that addresses the actual doubt.

Step 4: Move - Get commitment on the next step before they have time to retreat.
